Design a simple brand and homepage for the “Institute of Digital Risk” (IDR), an
industry-led training and deployment institute focused on digital, and AI risk.
Deliverables:
• 1 logo for IDR
• 1 responsive homepage (desktop + mobile)
1. Logo design task
Create a logo that:
• Uses a cube-inspired icon or geometric shape to suggest structure, risk
and resilience.
• Uses a primary colour palette of orange, white and black, with a clean,
minimalist tech/education feel.
• Works in two variants: icon-only, and icon + text “Institute of Digital Risk”.
• Is legible at small sizes (favicon / mobile header).
What to submit:
• PNG or SVG of both variants.
• A short note (3–4 sentences) explaining your design choices (colour,
typography, symbolism).
2. Homepage design and build
Create a single-page website (HTML/CSS/JS) for IDR that clearly explains who they are
and what they do.
Mandatory sections
1. Hero section
• Headline that reflects IDR’s mission, e.g. “Advancing the Future of Digital
Risk”.
• Subheading that explains that IDR trains and deploys digital risk
practitioners, combining academic collaboration and industry practice.
• Call-to-action button (e.g. “Explore Programs” or “Hire Talent”).
2. About IDR
• 1–2 short paragraphs explaining IDR as an industry-led training and
deployment institute for digital, cyber and technology risk roles in regulated,
high-consequence environments.
• Emphasise the link between academic insight (UK university partnership)
and real-world projects.
3. Service pillars / model
• Present the core model with at least three cards or blocks, for example:
• Academy (training / bootcamps for students and professionals).
• Innovation & Incubation (developing IP, future risk models, AI
governance).
• Advisory Services (helping organisations implement frameworks like NIST
ISO 27001, NIS2).
• Optionally show the “train – hire – innovate – deploy” pipeline visually
(timeline, steps or icons).
4. Community / who we serve
• Short section describing the community: students, early-career
professionals and practitioners in sectors like financial services and critical
infrastructure seeking upskilling in digital and cyber risk.
5. Contact / call to action
• Simple contact form (name, email, message) or a “Register interest” form.
• Placeholder email or button.
Technical requirements
• Use semantic HTML5 (header, nav, main, section, footer).
• Use responsive layout (CSS Flexbox or Grid) that works on mobile and
desktop.
• Implement a sticky navigation bar with smooth scrolling to sections.
• Use a clear, accessible colour contrast and web-safe or Google Fonts.
• Include basic hover states for buttons and nav links.
• No CSS frameworks (e.g. Bootstrap); only vanilla HTML/CSS/JS.
